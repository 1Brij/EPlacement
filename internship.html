Internships



Social Internships


Nonprofit organizations

Many nonprofit organizations offer internships that provide valuable hands-on experience in areas such as fundraising, community outreach, event planning, and advocacy. Examples of nonprofit organizations that offer internships include the American Red Cross, Habitat for Humanity, and the Make-A-Wish Foundation.

Government agencies

Local, state, and federal government agencies often offer internships that provide opportunities to work on projects related to public policy, social justice, and community development. Examples of government agencies that offer internships include the United States Department of Justice, the Environmental Protection Agency, and local city or county departments.

Rural

Social internships in rural areas could involve working with local communities, conducting needs assessments, developing and implementing programs, conducting research, and evaluating the impact of social programs.

Schools

Social internships in schools could involve working with teachers and students, developing and implementing education programs, providing academic support, conducting research, and evaluating the impact of educational interventions.
